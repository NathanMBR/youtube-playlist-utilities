  https://developers.google.com/terms/api-services-user-data-policy

Specifically:

  - We use Google API data only to provide and improve the features described
    in this policy (playlist management directly within the Application).
  - We do not use Google user data for advertising purposes.
  - We do not sell Google user data to any third party.
  - We do not allow any human to read Google user data unless (a) you have
    given express permission for us to do so, (b) it is necessary for security

    to operate the Application or as required by law.

================================================================================
1. WHAT DATA WE ACCESS
================================================================================

To provide its functionality, the Application requests access to the following
data through your Google account, using the scopes listed below:

Scope: https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.profile
  Purpose: Retrieve your basic Google profile information (name and profile
  picture) so the Application can display your identity while you are signed in.
  Data accessed: your Google account name, profile picture URL, and Google
  account ID.

Scope: https://www.googleapis.com/auth/youtube
  Purpose: Read your YouTube playlists to identify unavailable or non-public
  videos, and to allow you to remove or substitute those videos from your own
  playlists.
  Data accessed: playlist IDs, video IDs, video titles, video descriptions,
  channel names, privacy status (public / private / unlisted), video thumbnails,
  and video positions within your playlists.

The Application does not request access to any other Google services, your
Google contacts, Gmail, Google Drive, or any other personal data beyond what
is listed above.

================================================================================
2. HOW WE USE YOUR DATA
================================================================================

The data accessed through Google APIs is used exclusively for the following
user-facing features:

  a. Displaying your name and profile picture in the Application interface.

  b. Fetching the contents of a YouTube playlist you specify, so that
     unavailable or non-public videos can be identified and listed.

  c. Removing a video from your playlist at your explicit request.

  d. Replacing a video in your playlist with a substitute video at your
     explicit request, preserving the original position.

All data processing occurs locally on your device. No playlist content, video
metadata, or personal information is transmitted to any server operated by the
developer.

================================================================================
3. DATA STORAGE AND RETENTION
================================================================================

Authentication Token
  When you sign in with your Google account, Google returns an OAuth 2.0 access
  token. The Application stores this token in your device's local browser
  storage (localStorage) within the Application's sandboxed WebView. The token
  is:

    - Stored only on your local device.
    - Never transmitted to any server operated by the developer.
    - Automatically treated as expired and discarded once its validity period
      ends.
    - Immediately removed from local storage when you sign out of the
      Application.

Playlist and Video Data
  Playlist and video information fetched from the YouTube Data API is held only
  in the Application's memory while it is running. This data is not written to
  disk and is not retained between sessions.

No Server-Side Storage
  The Application has no backend server. We do not receive, store, log, or
  process any of your data on our infrastructure.

================================================================================
5. YOUR RIGHTS AND CONTROLS
================================================================================

You have full control over the data the Application accesses:

  Sign out: Signing out of the Application immediately removes your access
  token from local storage. The Application will not be able to access your
  Google data until you sign in again.

  Revoke access: You can revoke the Application's access to your Google
  account at any time through your Google Account security settings:
  https://myaccount.google.com/permissions
  Revoking access immediately invalidates any stored access token.

  Delete local data: Because all data is stored only in the Application's
  local storage, uninstalling the Application removes all locally stored data,
  including any cached access tokens.

  Data deletion request: If you believe the Application has retained any of
  your personal data, contact the developer at nathanmurillodeoliveira@gmail.com
  to request its deletion.

================================================================================
6. DATA SECURITY
================================================================================

We implement the following measures to protect your data:

  - All communication with Google APIs is conducted over HTTPS.
  - OAuth access tokens are stored only in the Application's sandboxed
    WebView storage and are not accessible to other applications on your device.
  - The Application requests only the minimum Google API scopes necessary to
    provide its features.
  - No user data is transmitted to or stored on any server operated by the
    developer.
